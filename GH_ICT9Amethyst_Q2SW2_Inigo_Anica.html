MY TOP 5 FAVORITE DOG BREEDS

Breed Description
Siberian Husky The Siberian Husky is a working dog breed that originated in Siberia and was raised by the Chukchi people and were valued as sled dogs and companions. The graceful dog has erect ears, a dense soft coat of fur, may grow up to 20-24 inches (51-61 cm) tall and may weigh from 35-60 pounds (16-27 kg). It's usually black and white, tan, or gray. The breed is known for its intelligence and gentle temperament.
Golden Retriever One of the most popular dog breeds is the Golden Retriever because it is the ideal family dog. It's very lively, loyal, friendly, and is also good with children. Its golden brown coat also makes up for its fantastic appearance. The dog breed was originally bred in Scotland between 1840 and 1890 and was treated as a hunting dog. Now, the Golden Retriever is used as a guide dog, tracking dog, and a therapy dog.
Chow Chow The Chow Chow is known as the lion of the dog's world due to its lion-like features, specifically the long refined mane around its neck and a scowling expression. Chow Chows are loyal and friendly to their immediate families but are suspicious of strangers. They make great apartment dogs due to their clean and silent nature. An adult Chow Chow can weigh 45-70 pounds and can stand at a height of 17-20 inches. Their lion-like manes make them look even larger and more intimidating.
Pomeranian Pomeranians, in contrast to their small stature, have big personalities. They're alert, confident, intelligent, and have the demeanor of a much more formidable canine. According to Pomeranian owners, Poms (coined term by Pomeranian lovers) are very easy to train not only because they are smart but also because they love learning new tricks and making their owners happy. Pomeranians may grow upto 6-7 inches and may weigh 3-7 pounds.
Shih Tzu The Shih Tzu is a longhaired, active, and alert little dog. The dog breed is short-legged, has a short muzzle, and a heavily haired tail. The Shih Tzu was first developed in Tibet about a thousand years ago. Due to its size, the Shih Tzu strives better in warm climates and enjoys playing games.
